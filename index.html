Our core belief

Everyone deserves a fresh start.

A main focus of NPA Support Services is supporting participants to move forward from historical behaviours, incidents and labels. A person’s history should be understood — but it should not always define who they are, what they are capable of, or the opportunities available to them.

“Behaviour is often a form of communication. Our role is to understand what a person may be trying to say, and to support them to be seen as more than their past.”

Advocacy & dignity

Recognising each participant’s strengths, potential and right to be treated with respect.

Reducing restrictive practices

Working toward safer, less restrictive supports wherever possible.

Building capacity

Practical, realistic strategies families and support teams can use every day.

Common questions

Frequently asked questions

A few things people often ask. If your question isn’t here, please get in touch — we’re happy to help.

What is Positive Behaviour Support?
Positive Behaviour Support (PBS) helps us understand why behaviours of concern are happening and what support a person needs to feel safe, understood and included. Behaviour is often a form of communication — our role is to identify what a person may be trying to communicate, what is causing distress, and what practical supports can reduce risk and improve quality of life.
What areas do you service?
NPA Support Services works with NDIS participants across Melbourne and surrounding areas, and Victoria more broadly. If you’re unsure whether we can support your location, please get in touch and we’ll let you know.
Who can make a referral?
Anyone with the participant’s consent or authority can make a referral — including participants themselves, families, carers, support coordinators, providers, schools, SIL providers, day programs and allied health professionals. When you submit a referral you’ll be asked to confirm you have consent to share the information.
What NDIS funding do I need?
Positive Behaviour Support is generally funded under Improved Relationships, while Support Coordination and Specialist Support Coordination sit under the relevant Support Coordination funding in your plan. You can be self-managed, plan-managed or agency-managed. If you’re not sure what your plan includes, contact us and we can help review this with you.
Can I access more than one service at once?
Yes. Many participants access a combination — for example Positive Behaviour Support alongside Support Coordination. When you refer, you can select PBS, Support Coordination, Specialist Support Coordination, or a combination, and we’ll talk through what best suits your needs.
What happens after I submit a referral?
Once your referral is received, NPA Support Services will review the information and contact you to discuss suitability, availability and next steps. If we aren’t the right fit, we’ll do our best to point you in a helpful direction.
